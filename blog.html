Understanding Weather Forecasting🌤️

🧠 How does your phone know it’s going to rain tomorrow?
Weather forecasting is the science of predicting atmospheric conditions like temperature, rainfall, humidity, and wind for a specific location and time. While it may seem like guesswork, it’s actually backed by powerful data collection and modeling systems.


🔍 How Forecasting Works

Forecasting uses Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP) — complex mathematical models that simulate the atmosphere using current weather data.
Key data sources include:
🛰 Satellites – Observe clouds, storms, ocean temps.
🎈 Weather Balloons – Measure pressure, humidity, wind at high altitudes.
🌍 Ground Stations – Track local temperature, pressure, rainfall.
📡 Radar Systems – Detect precipitation and wind movement.

📊 Fact: ECMWF uses 9 million+ data points daily for forecasts 🌍

⚙️ Tech Behind It

Supercomputers: Process millions of global observations daily.
Forecast Models: GFS (Global), ECMWF (Europe), IMD (India).
AI in Forecasting: Tools like Google’s GraphCast are improving accuracy with faster simulations.

Cyclone Detection: India’s Satellite Strategy🌪️

🌀 How India tracks dangerous cyclones like Biparjoy and Fani.

India’s vast coastline faces frequent cyclones. Thankfully, agencies like IMD and ISRO use satellite data from INSAT, SCATSAT, and OceanSat missions to issue early warnings.

📊 Fact: IMD’s cyclone predictions have improved accuracy by over 95% since the 1990s.


🌐 Key Satellites:

INSAT-3D (Infrared imaging)
SCATSAT-1 (Wind & storm speed)
Doppler Radar (installed along coasts)
Compact UV Umbrella
